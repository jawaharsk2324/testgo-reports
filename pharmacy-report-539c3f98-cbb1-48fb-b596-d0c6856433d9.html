Additional Information

Escherichia Coli and Pseudomonas Aeruginosa can be pathogenic when found in urine samples. The primary drug, nitrofurantoin, was selected based on the bacterial UTI classification and organism distribution. Resistance gene TetM was detected, which may affect treatment options. IV drugs cefiderocol and cefazidime-avibactam were selected to address resistance patterns and provide broader coverage.

Treatment Timing Guidelines

Asymptomatic bacteriuria does not typically need treatment, and microbe detection may not indicate infection. This bacterial UTI is typically treated for 3-5 days. However, detected resistance genes may require extended treatment duration and combination therapy.

Special Considerations

Patients may have colonized microbes, so a positive specimen does not necessarily mean infection. Oral drugs ciprofloxacin, trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole, and fosfomycin provide standard coverage. IV drugs cefiderocol and cefazidime-avibactam address specific resistance: cefiderocol targets TetM. Antibiotics should be used with caution as drug failure is possible.
